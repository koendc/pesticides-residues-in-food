What these figures show

Three ways to understand pesticide levels

Pesticide load score adds up how close each detected substance is to its legal limit — the higher the score, the more residue was found relative to what is permitted. Pesticides detected counts how many different pesticide substances were found in a sample. Legal limit exceedance rate shows how often a single measurement was above the permitted maximum (MRL).

How to read this

No single number tells the whole story

A food can contain many different pesticides without any single one breaching the legal limit. Conversely, a low average can hide occasional spikes above the limit. The three sections below are designed to be read together.

Methodology

How these figures are calculated

Legal limit exceedances

A result is counted as exceeding the legal limit when the measured residue is above the Maximum Residue Level (MRL) set for that substance and food combination.

Pesticide load score

For each food sample, all individual substance levels are added together as a fraction of their respective limits. A score of 1.0 means the combined residues equal the sum of all permitted levels. The number of distinct pesticides found in each sample is also recorded.

Organic vs conventional

Each food sample is labelled as either organically or conventionally grown. Both production types are analysed separately so they can be compared directly.
